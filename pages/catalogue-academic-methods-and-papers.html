Catalogue - academic methods and papers

This page catalogues foundational and policy-relevant literature for SMCE, Equity analysis, stakeholder-sensitive appraisal, and Sensitivity and robustness analysis. The focus is on sources that are useful for European Commission-style impact assessment, social multi-criteria evaluation, and transparent comparison of policy options under value conflict and uncertainty.

How to use this catalogue

  • Use the SMCE and MCDA entries to ground the overall appraisal logic.
  • Use the stakeholder participation entries when designing consultation and group representation.
  • Use the equity/distribution entries when clarifying what “fairness” or “distributional impact” means in the appraisal.
  • Use the sensitivity/robustness entries when testing whether rankings or recommendations are stable.
  • Use the policy manuals and EC guidance entries when translating academic methods into public-policy workflow.

A. SMCE and MCDA foundations

1) Social multi-criteria evaluation: Methodological foundations and operational consequences

  • Authors: Giuseppe Munda
  • Year: 2004
  • Venue/type: European Journal of Operational Research, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0377-2217(03)00369-2
  • Relevance: Core methodological statement of SMCE. Explains why policy evaluation should accommodate incommensurable values, conflicting social perspectives, and non-reducibility to a single metric.

2) Social Multi-Criteria Evaluation for a Sustainable Economy

  • Authors: Giuseppe Munda
  • Year: 2008
  • Venue/type: Springer, book
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-540-73703-2
  • Relevance: Main book-length treatment of SMCE. Especially useful for linking multicriteria reasoning with sustainability, social choice, participation, and public decision support.

3) Weak comparability of values as a foundation for ecological economics

  • Authors: Joan Martinez-Alier, Giuseppe Munda, John O'Neill
  • Year: 1998
  • Venue/type: Ecological Economics, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0921-8009(97)00120-1
  • Relevance: Foundational argument for plural valuation. Important background for why SMCE is preferable when policy impacts cannot be meaningfully collapsed into one common unit.

4) Multiple-Criteria Decision Analysis: An Integrated Approach

  • Authors: Valerie Belton, Theodor J. Stewart
  • Year: 2002
  • Venue/type: Springer, book
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4615-1495-4
  • Relevance: Standard MCDA reference covering problem structuring, scoring, weighting, outranking, and sensitivity. Useful as a bridge between broad MCDA literature and SMCE applications.

5) Multicriteria Methodology for Decision Aiding

  • Authors: Bernard Roy
  • Year: 1996
  • Venue/type: Springer, book
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4757-2500-1
  • Relevance: Classic decision-aiding text from the European MCDA tradition. Especially relevant for understanding decision support, outranking, imperfect knowledge, and the distinction between aiding and “solving”.

6) Decisions with Multiple Objectives: Preferences and Value Trade-Offs

  • Authors: Ralph L. Keeney, Howard Raiffa
  • Year: 1976
  • Venue/type: Wiley, book
  • URL/DOI: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Decisions+with+Multiple+Objectives%3A+Preferences+and+Value+Tradeoffs-p-9780521438834
  • Relevance: Foundational value-focused decision analysis reference. Useful for policy contexts where criteria need to be derived from objectives and trade-offs need explicit discussion.

7) Multiple Criteria Decision Analysis: State of the Art Surveys

  • Editors: José Figueira, Salvatore Greco, Matthias Ehrgott
  • Year: 2005
  • Venue/type: Springer, edited volume
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/b100605
  • Relevance: Broad survey volume on MCDA methods. Good orientation source for comparing outranking, utility-based, interactive, and uncertainty-handling approaches.

B. Policy appraisal and public-sector application

8) Multi-criteria analysis: a manual

  • Authors: John Dodgson, Mike Spackman, Alan Pearman, L. David Phillips
  • Year: 2009
  • Venue/type: UK Department for Communities and Local Government, policy manual/report
  • URL/DOI: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/multi-criteria-analysis-manual-for-making-government-policy
  • Relevance: One of the most practical public-policy MCDA manuals in English. Very useful for structuring options, criteria, scoring, weighting, and sensitivity checks in a government appraisal setting.

9) Better Regulation Guidelines and Better Regulation Toolbox

  • Authors: European Commission
  • Year: current guidance page; toolbox updated periodically
  • Venue/type: policy guidance / toolbox
  • URL/DOI: https://commission.europa.eu/law/law-making-process/planning-and-proposing-law/better-regulation/better-regulation-guidelines-and-toolbox_en
  • Relevance: Not an academic source, but directly relevant to EC impact assessment practice. Essential for aligning SMCE outputs with official Commission process expectations, consultation requirements, and reporting standards.

10) Handbook on Constructing Composite Indicators: Methodology and User Guide

  • Authors: Michela Nardo, Michaela Saisana, Andrea Saltelli, Stefano Tarantola, Anders Hoffman, Enrico Giovannini
  • Year: 2008
  • Venue/type: OECD/JRC, handbook
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1787/9789264043466-en
  • Relevance: Important for weighting, aggregation, uncertainty, and sensitivity in indicator-based appraisal. Particularly relevant where policy comparison uses dashboards or composite measures.

C. Stakeholder participation and participatory appraisal

11) Stakeholder participation for environmental management: A literature review

  • Authors: Mark S. Reed
  • Year: 2008
  • Venue/type: Biological Conservation, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.biocon.2008.07.014
  • Relevance: Widely cited review of why and how stakeholder participation affects decision quality, legitimacy, learning, and implementation. Highly relevant for designing stakeholder-sensitive SMCE.

12) Public Participation Methods: A Framework for Evaluation

  • Authors: Gene Rowe, Lynn J. Frewer
  • Year: 2000
  • Venue/type: Science, Technology, & Human Values, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1177/016224390002500101
  • Relevance: Provides a practical evaluation framework for participation methods. Useful when choosing between surveys, workshops, focus groups, or deliberative processes.

13) What Is a Good Public Participation Process? Five Perspectives from the Public

  • Authors: Thomas Webler, Seth Tuler, Rob Krueger
  • Year: 2001
  • Venue/type: Environmental Management, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s002670010160
  • Relevance: Helpful for defining quality criteria for participation from the public’s perspective, including fairness, competence, and legitimacy.

14) Multiple-criteria decision aid: Some epistemological considerations

  • Authors: Giuseppe Munda
  • Year: 1993
  • Venue/type: Journal of Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1002/mcda.4020020106
  • Relevance: Useful conceptual source on how MCDA handles knowledge, values, and decision support. Relevant when combining technical evidence with stakeholder judgement.

D. Equity, fairness, and distributional analysis

15) Equality of What?

  • Authors: Amartya Sen
  • Year: 1979
  • Venue/type: Tanner Lecture / foundational lecture
  • URL/DOI: https://tannerlectures.utah.edu/_resources/documents/a-to-z/s/sen80.pdf
  • Relevance: Canonical source for asking which space of evaluation matters for equity: utility, resources, capabilities, or something else. Very useful for clarifying what distributional analysis should measure.

16) Well-Being and Fair Distribution: Beyond Cost-Benefit Analysis

  • Authors: Matthew D. Adler
  • Year: 2011
  • Venue/type: Oxford University Press, book
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780195384994.001.0001
  • Relevance: Major normative treatment of fair policy evaluation beyond conventional CBA. Useful for connecting welfare economics, distributional concern, and policy appraisal.

17) Unfair inequalities in health and health care

  • Authors: Marc Fleurbaey, Erik Schokkaert
  • Year: 2009
  • Venue/type: Journal of Health Economics, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jhealeco.2008.07.016
  • Relevance: A strong example of formal fairness analysis in applied policy. Useful as a model for distinguishing ethically relevant from ethically irrelevant inequalities.

18) Cost-Benefit Analysis and Distributional Weights: An Overview

  • Authors: Matthew D. Adler
  • Year: 2013
  • Venue/type: working paper / overview
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.2313388
  • Relevance: Clear overview of why and how distributional weights enter policy appraisal. Helpful for comparing MCDA-style equity treatment with monetized distributional weighting approaches.

19) Distributional Effects of Environmental Policy

  • Editors: Ysé Serret, Nick Johnstone
  • Year: 2006
  • Venue/type: OECD, edited volume
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1787/9789264025530-en
  • Relevance: Focused reference on how environmental policy affects groups differently. Useful for policy teams that need examples of distributional incidence and compensation issues.

E. Sensitivity, uncertainty, and robustness

20) A Sensitivity Analysis Approach for Some Deterministic Multi-Criteria Decision-Making Methods

  • Authors: Evangelos Triantaphyllou, Alfonso Sánchez
  • Year: 1997
  • Venue/type: Decision Sciences, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-5915.1997.tb01306.x
  • Relevance: Classic reference on how small changes in parameters can alter MCDM outcomes. Good starting point for local sensitivity checks on weights and scores.

21) Sensitivity Analysis in Practice: A Guide to Assessing Scientific Models

  • Authors: Andrea Saltelli, Stefano Tarantola, Francesca Campolongo, Marco Ratto
  • Year: 2004
  • Venue/type: Wiley, book
  • URL/DOI: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/book/10.1002/0470870958
  • Relevance: Practical guide to sensitivity analysis concepts and workflows. Useful when policy appraisal depends on model assumptions, uncertain inputs, or scenario variation.

22) Global Sensitivity Analysis: The Primer

  • Authors: Andrea Saltelli, Marco Ratto, Terry Andres, Francesca Campolongo, Jessica Cariboni, Debora Gatelli, Michaela Saisana, Stefano Tarantola
  • Year: 2007
  • Venue/type: Wiley, monograph
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1002/9780470725184
  • Relevance: Standard reference for variance-based and global sensitivity analysis. Particularly important when robustness must be demonstrated beyond one-at-a-time parameter changes.

23) SMAA-2: Stochastic Multicriteria Acceptability Analysis for Group Decision Making

  • Authors: Risto Lahdelma, Pekka Salminen
  • Year: 2001
  • Venue/type: Operations Research, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1287/opre.49.3.444.11220
  • Relevance: Key method for dealing with uncertain or partially known weights and group preferences. Highly relevant where stakeholder weights are contested or incomplete.

24) Robustness in Multi-criteria Decision Aiding

  • Authors: Hassene Aissi, Bernard Roy
  • Year: 2010
  • Venue/type: book chapter in Trends in Multiple Criteria Decision Analysis
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4419-5904-1_4
  • Relevance: Important conceptual source on robustness in MCDA. Helps distinguish stable conclusions from fragile ones when assumptions are revised.

25) Sensitivity and Robustness Analyses in Social Multi-Criteria Evaluation of Public Policies

  • Authors: Ivano Azzini, Giuseppe Munda
  • Year: 2025
  • Venue/type: Journal of Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis, journal article
  • URL/DOI: https://doi.org/10.1002/mcda.70006
  • Relevance: Directly targeted at SMCE in public policy. Particularly relevant for the current wiki because it addresses how to test robustness in a specifically social and policy-oriented multicriteria setting.

Quick synthesis across the literature

  • SMCE grows out of the recognition that policy appraisal involves plural values, conflicting stakeholder perspectives, and incomplete comparability.
  • Public-sector MCDA literature emphasizes transparent structuring of objectives, criteria, scores, weights, and assumptions rather than hidden optimization.
  • Participation literature shows that stakeholder involvement improves legitimacy and problem framing, but only if representation, transparency, and method fit are handled carefully.
  • Equity literature clarifies that distributional analysis is not just “who wins and loses”, but also depends on the evaluative space used: welfare, resources, capabilities, rights, or group-relative impact.
  • Robustness literature shows that policy rankings should never be treated as final without checking sensitivity to weights, data uncertainty, model form, and stakeholder disagreement.

Suggested next reading path

  1. Start with Munda (2004) and Munda (2008) for the logic of SMCE.
  2. Use Belton & Stewart (2002), Roy (1996), and Dodgson et al. (2009) for operational MCDA design.
  3. Use Reed (2008), Rowe & Frewer (2000), and Webler et al. (2001) for consultation design.
  4. Use Sen (1979), Adler (2011), and Serret & Johnstone (2006) for equity framing.
  5. Use Triantaphyllou & Sánchez (1997), Saltelli et al. (2004; 2007), Lahdelma & Salminen (2001), and Aissi & Roy (2010) for robustness checks.
