Better Regulation

Better Regulation is the European Commission’s framework for preparing, revising, and evaluating EU initiatives in a way that is evidence-based, transparent, and open to stakeholder input.

For this wiki, Better Regulation is the main official process environment within which SMCE and SOCRATES would need to operate.

What Better Regulation is for

Across the official Commission sources, Better Regulation is presented as a quality framework for policymaking. Its purpose is to improve the design of EU action by requiring: - clear problem definition - explicit objectives - comparison of realistic policy options - evidence gathering proportional to the importance of the initiative - stakeholder consultation - attention to implementation and simplification - later evaluation of whether the intervention worked

The framework is therefore not only about ex ante impact assessment. It also covers calls for evidence, consultations, evaluations, fitness checks, and scrutiny of analytical quality.

Main building blocks

1. Political and strategic framing

The Commission’s 2021 communication on Better Regulation presents the current agenda as a way to make law-making more joined-up, future-oriented, and easier to navigate. It connects Better Regulation to strategic foresight, burden reduction, and improved participation.

2. Operational guidance

The practical operating rules are set out in the Better Regulation catalogue and especially in the 2021 Better Regulation Guidelines and the Toolbox.

Those sources define how Commission services should: - identify the problem - establish the baseline - formulate objectives - design policy options - assess impacts - consult stakeholders - explain proportionality choices - prepare monitoring and evaluation arrangements

3. Public participation

The Have Your Say system and related consultation pages are the official route for collecting public and stakeholder feedback. This matters for SMCE because stakeholder input is not an optional extra in the Commission model; it is built into the policy cycle.

4. Quality control

The Regulatory Scrutiny Board is the Commission’s internal quality-control body for major impact assessments and for selected evaluations and fitness checks. Better Regulation therefore includes both methods and institutional review.

Why Better Regulation matters for SMCE

The existing local page Policy making process - impact assessment already shows that SMCE is best understood as an analytical contribution inside the standard Commission process, not as a substitute for it.

From the official sources, SMCE appears most compatible with Better Regulation in the following places: - option comparison after the problem and objectives are defined - criteria design and evidence structuring - explicit treatment of trade-offs across multiple dimensions - stakeholder-informed equity analysis - sensitivity and robustness analysis - support for drafting a defensible impact assessment narrative

In other words, Better Regulation defines the process requirements, while SMCE can help structure the comparison of options within those requirements.

Consultation under Better Regulation

Official Commission material consistently treats consultation as a normal part of policy preparation rather than a late-stage validation exercise.

For SMCE-related work, that has two implications: - early consultation can help identify relevant dimensions, objectives, and criteria - later structured consultation can provide comparable stakeholder judgements on policy options

This aligns closely with Stakeholder input guidance, which translates the consultation logic into SMCE-specific operational steps.

Evaluation and learning

Better Regulation also extends beyond ex ante analysis. The framework links new initiatives to later monitoring, evaluation, and where relevant fitness checks. This is important because a robust ex ante comparison should also anticipate what evidence will later be needed to assess effectiveness, efficiency, coherence, relevance, and EU added value.

For wiki purposes, that means SMCE-related designs should ideally be traceable into later evaluation questions, not only into initial option ranking.

Practical takeaways

  • Better Regulation is the official process shell around Commission policy assessment.
  • The Guidelines and Toolbox are more important than ad hoc presentations or project notes.
  • Stakeholder consultation is an integral process requirement.
  • Impact assessment is only one part of a wider policy cycle that also includes evaluation.
  • The Regulatory Scrutiny Board is central for analytical quality control.
  • SMCE and SOCRATES are most useful when framed as methods that help satisfy Better Regulation expectations for structured option comparison.
