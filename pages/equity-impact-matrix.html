Equity Impact Matrix

Preferred canonical page: Equity impact matrix guidance.

This alias catches the shorter name often used in slides, notes, or spreadsheet references for the stakeholder-by-option matrix at the center of the equity analysis workflow.

What the term usually means

In this wiki, the equity impact matrix is the structured table that records how different stakeholder groups evaluate each policy option, including the baseline. It is not just a template artifact; it is the operational bridge between consultation or expert judgement and the later Equity analysis.

For the step-by-step explanation of how to build, populate, and use that matrix, go to Equity impact matrix guidance.
