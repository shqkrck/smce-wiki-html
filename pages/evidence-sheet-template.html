Evidence sheet template

This page is a working template for recording one evidence item in a way that is usable for the AI-enabled SMCE method, reviewable by humans, and easy to trace into the Assessment structure and multi-criteria impact matrix, Rationale note template, and Uncertainty register template.

Use one page or one repeated block per evidence item. Do not merge multiple unrelated sources into one sheet.

When to use this template

  • Use it when a source is important enough to support a criterion, option assessment, implementation judgement, stakeholder claim, or uncertainty entry.
  • Use it for official sources, studies, expert inputs, stakeholder submissions, model outputs, legal analysis, and internal analytical notes.
  • Do not use it for casual background reading that does not affect the analysis.

Required completion rules

  • Every sheet must identify the source precisely enough that a reviewer can find it again.
  • Every substantive claim must distinguish between what the source says and how the analyst is interpreting it.
  • If the source is weak, partial, contradictory, or outdated, say so explicitly.
  • If AI helped produce the draft sheet, the AI assistance and human validation must be logged.

Usage guidance

  • Keep the sheet factual first, interpretive second.
  • If one source supports several criteria, either duplicate the sheet with criterion-specific interpretation or keep one master sheet and link separate rationale notes.
  • If evidence quality is weak but decision-relevant, do not discard it silently. Record it, caveat it, and link it to the uncertainty register.
  • If a source is used to justify a score or descriptor, the exact supporting line or data point should be findable within minutes.

Minimum acceptance check

Before marking an evidence sheet as reviewed, confirm that:

  • the source can be located,
  • the relevant claim is quoted or precisely referenced,
  • the quality judgement is explicit,
  • limitations are visible,
  • any AI assistance has been logged,
  • a reviewer has checked the interpretation.
