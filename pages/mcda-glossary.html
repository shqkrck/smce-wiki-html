MCDA glossary

A compact reference for key terms used across this wiki.

Aggregation

The rule used to combine criterion-level information into an overall comparison, ranking, or recommendation. Different aggregation rules allow different degrees of compensation between strong and weak performances.

Baseline

The no-policy-change or business-as-usual scenario against which policy options are compared. In this wiki, the baseline should usually be included explicitly in both the multi-criteria comparison and the equity analysis. See Policy making process - impact assessment and Equity impact matrix guidance.

Compensability

The extent to which poor performance on one criterion can be offset by good performance on another. High compensability is common in weighted-sum models; lower compensability is typical of some outranking approaches.

Criterion

A specific aspect used to compare options. Criteria operationalize broader objectives or dimensions and should be distinct, relevant, and interpretable. See SMCE.

Deliberative appraisal

An approach to evaluation that treats discussion, justification, and stakeholder learning as part of the appraisal process, not just a supplement to it. This is central to the “social” dimension of SMCE.

Dimension

A broad impact area or evaluative category, such as economic, social, environmental, or administrative effects. Dimensions are often broken down into objectives and criteria. See SMCE.

Distributional effects

Differences in how benefits, costs, risks, or burdens fall across stakeholder groups, territories, or time periods. Distributional effects are central to Equity analysis.

Equity analysis

A structured assessment of how policy options affect or are evaluated by different stakeholder groups, with attention to fairness, incidence, and conflict across groups. See Equity analysis.

Equity impact matrix

A table crossing stakeholder groups with policy options and recording each group’s evaluation of each option. It is the core operational tool for stakeholder-based equity analysis. See Equity impact matrix guidance.

Indicator

A qualitative or quantitative descriptor used to inform the assessment of a criterion. An indicator does not have to be monetary, and in SMCE it may be numerical, ordinal, or descriptive.

MCDA

Multi-criteria decision analysis: a family of methods for comparing alternatives across multiple criteria when no single metric is sufficient.

Normalization

A procedure for transforming criterion values into a comparable scale before aggregation or comparison. Different normalization choices can affect results and should be documented.

Option

A policy alternative being assessed. Good MCDA requires options to be defined clearly enough for evidence gathering, stakeholder consultation, and comparison.

Outranking

A family of MCDA approaches that asks whether one option can be judged at least as good as another, given the evidence and preference model, without requiring full compensation across all criteria.

Preference elicitation

The process of gathering judgements about the relative importance of criteria or the attractiveness of options. This may involve surveys, workshops, interviews, rankings, ratings, or structured expert judgement. See Stakeholder input guidance.

Qualitative scale

A verbal or symbolic scale used to represent evaluations when precise measurement is not available or not appropriate. Examples include Very good to Very bad or +++ to ---. See Equity impact matrix guidance.

Ranking

An ordering of options from more to less preferred. A ranking may be complete or partial, stable or sensitivity-dependent.

Robustness analysis

Assessment of whether the main appraisal conclusion remains defensible across plausible changes in assumptions, data, weights, stakeholder groupings, or model choices. See Sensitivity and robustness analysis.

Scoring

The assignment of values, ratings, or qualitative judgements to describe how an option performs on a criterion.

Sensitivity analysis

Testing how results change when one or more inputs vary, such as weights, scores, thresholds, or scenario assumptions. See Sensitivity and robustness analysis.

SMCE

Social multi-criteria evaluation: a branch of MCDA designed for contexts with plural values, multiple social actors, and contested policy choices. It places special emphasis on stakeholder perspectives, deliberation, and equity. See SMCE.

Stakeholder

Any actor, group, or constituency that is affected by the policy, can influence it, helps implement it, or has relevant knowledge about it. See Stakeholder mapping and Stakeholder input guidance.

Stakeholder mapping

The structured identification and characterization of relevant actors, including their relationship to the policy, likely impacts, influence, and representational relevance. See Stakeholder mapping.

Trade-off

A situation in which improving performance on one criterion or for one group comes at the expense of another. One of the main purposes of MCDA is to make trade-offs explicit rather than hiding them in a single metric.

Weight

A parameter expressing the relative importance assigned to a criterion or objective within a given model. Weights are value-laden and should be tested for sensitivity. See Sensitivity and robustness analysis.
