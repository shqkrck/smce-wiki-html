Wiki audit memo

Strengths

  • Good core spine around SMCE, Policy making process - impact assessment, EC impact assessment, Better Regulation, and Regulatory Scrutiny Board.
  • Strong coverage of two important operational subtopics: stakeholder input and equity analysis.
  • Source catalogues are useful and mostly well aligned with topic pages.
  • The AI pages are separated sensibly into workflow/use-case material and governance/safeguards material.

Issues

1. Many likely-missing pages are linked as if they already exist

Most important dangling or likely-missing wikilinks:

  • SOCRATES
  • Impact Assessment / EC impact assessment naming split
  • Social Multi-Criteria Evaluation (SMCE) / SMCE naming split
  • Multi-Criteria Analysis, Multi-criteria decision analysis, Sensitivity Analysis
  • Equity Impact Matrix vs Equity impact matrix guidance
  • Better Regulation Guidelines
  • SOCRATES Manual
  • Stakeholder consultation, Better Regulation stakeholder consultation, Stakeholder mapping
  • Assessment structure and multi-criteria impact matrix

2. Naming is inconsistent

  • Acronym page exists as SMCE, but some pages link to Social Multi-Criteria Evaluation (SMCE).
  • EC impact assessment coexists conceptually with missing Impact Assessment.
  • Equity impact matrix guidance is used alongside missing Equity Impact Matrix.
  • MCDA terms vary: “Multi-Criteria Analysis”, “Multi-criteria decision analysis”, and generic “multi-criteria analysis”.
  • File naming style is mixed: most pages use spaced titles, but AI-enabled-SMCE-technical-proposal is hyphenated.

3. A few pages are underlinked or isolated

  • AI-enabled-SMCE-technical-proposal only appears to be linked from the home page.
  • Catalogue - academic methods and papers has very few inbound links despite being foundational.
  • SMCE Wiki Home has no backlinks and works more as a loose note than a maintained index.

4. Some topic boundaries are blurry

  • Better Regulation, EC impact assessment, and Policy making process - impact assessment overlap substantially in explaining the EC process context.
  • AI governance for impact assessment and AI-assisted policy analysis are distinct, but they would benefit from clearer “use this page for…” framing and more cross-links to the technical proposal.

5. Core topic coverage has a few obvious gaps

The wiki repeatedly points to topics that are central enough to deserve dedicated pages, especially:

  • SOCRATES as tool/software/method environment
  • Better Regulation Guidelines / Toolbox as the operational rule set
  • Stakeholder mapping
  • Assessment structure / impact matrix design
  • A canonical MCDA page or glossary page for method terms

Recommended fixes

  1. Create a canonical page for SOCRATES first, then update all SOCRATES-related dangling links to point to it.
  2. Standardise canonical names and retarget aliases consistently:
  • use SMCE as the canonical page
  • use EC impact assessment as the canonical page unless you intentionally want a broader Impact Assessment page
  • use Equity impact matrix guidance or create Equity Impact Matrix and make one the clear canonical target
  • choose one MCDA label and use it consistently
  1. Replace dangling links that do not need their own page with existing pages or plain text. Likely candidates: Social Multi-Criteria Evaluation (SMCE), Impact Assessment, Sensitivity Analysis, Better Regulation Guidelines, SOCRATES Manual.
  2. Add backlinks and cross-links for underlinked pages:
  • link AI-enabled-SMCE-technical-proposal from the two AI pages
  • link Catalogue - academic methods and papers from SMCE, Equity analysis, and Sensitivity and robustness analysis more prominently
  • turn SMCE Wiki Home into a real index page with sections for core concepts, process pages, operational guidance, AI pages, and catalogues
  1. Reduce process-page overlap by tightening roles:
  • Better Regulation = umbrella framework
  • EC impact assessment = formal EC product/process
  • Policy making process - impact assessment = slide-derived operational walkthrough showing where SMCE/SOCRATES fit
  1. If you do want the missing linked topics as standalone pages, keep them short and hub-like rather than long essays.
