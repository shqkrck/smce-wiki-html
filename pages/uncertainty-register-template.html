Uncertainty register template

This page is an operational template for maintaining the uncertainty register required by the AI-enabled SMCE method. It turns the guidance in Uncertainty register into a controlled working artifact for project execution and review.

Use one shared register for the whole file or demonstrator. Do not keep parallel hidden lists by workstream.

What this register must do

  • keep material uncertainty visible from evidence collection through reporting
  • connect uncertainties to criteria, options, stakeholder groups, and outputs
  • assign handling actions and owners
  • show whether uncertainty was tested, accepted, or escalated
  • preserve traceability for reviewers and the demonstrator audit trail

Required completion rules

  • Record only decision-relevant uncertainties.
  • Every material uncertainty must have an owner and a handling strategy.
  • If uncertainty affects a score, descriptor, ranking statement, or stakeholder interpretation, link it to the affected artifact.
  • Do not close an uncertainty without recording why it is considered resolved or accepted.

Recommended handling rules

  • High-materiality uncertainties should normally trigger either targeted evidence follow-up, a scenario, a sensitivity test, or an explicit escalation decision.
  • If uncertainty is mainly distributional, it should be visible in equity analysis rather than folded into an average statement.
  • If uncertainty is unresolved but material, the final deliverable should say whether conclusions remain robust, conditional, or fragile.
  • If uncertainty comes from methodological choice rather than evidence weakness, record that distinction explicitly.

Review checkpoints

Use the register actively at these points: 1. After initial evidence collection 2. After criteria and matrix structure stabilise 3. Before any comparative conclusion is stated 4. Before demonstrator evaluation 5. Before final integrated package assembly

Traffic-light shortcut for project control

  • Red = high materiality and unresolved
  • Amber = material but being handled
  • Green = tested or accepted with transparent residual caveat

Usage guidance

  • Keep descriptions concrete. “Data uncertain” is too vague; specify what data, for which option or criterion, and why it matters.
  • Distinguish between analytical uncertainty and project-management inconvenience.
  • Use this register to drive action, not just to record concerns.
  • Preserve old entries for traceability even when status changes.

Minimum acceptance check

Before a review gate, confirm that: - material uncertainties have been logged, - owners and actions are assigned, - links to affected outputs are present, - unresolved items are visible, - closure decisions are justified.
