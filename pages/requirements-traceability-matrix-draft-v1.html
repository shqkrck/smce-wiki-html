Requirements traceability matrix - draft v1

Document metadata - Document type: First-draft execution control page - Status: Draft v1 populated from proposal and contract reread context - Intended use: Working control spine for the integrated methodology + demonstrator package - Primary source set: - AI-enabled SMCE technical proposal - Next steps after contract reread - /home/jhr/.hermes/cache/extracted_docs/doc_eb39fe39d8af_EC_JRC_IPR_2026_VLVP_1348_Technical_Proposal_Briar_Hamsfest_plain.docx.txt - Integrated deliverable structure - AI intervention-point register - Human oversight and approval gates - Contract end date referenced in the source material: 22 May 2026

Purpose of this draft

This version is not a blank template. It is a first executable control matrix for the project as actually proposed: a methodology-first, governance-first package with a synthetic demonstrator and bounded AI support for preparatory SMCE work.

This page translates the proposal commitments into: - interpreted requirements - concrete delivery responses - named working artifacts - planned evidence for acceptance - unresolved items that should become JRC decision requests rather than hidden ambiguity

Working package assumptions used in this draft

These assumptions are introduced so the project package can operate as a real first draft rather than a generic shell.

  1. The proof-of-concept remains file-based and replayable, not integrated into live EC systems.
  2. The demonstrator will use one synthetic Better Regulation-style case with a manageable option set, evidence corpus, and review trail.
  3. Working synthetic case assumption for planning only: - Topic: improving access to targeted household energy support through different eligibility and delivery models. - Baseline: application-based access with fragmented information, burdensome proof requirements, and substantial non-take-up among eligible households. - Option A: improved information and assisted application support within the current model. - Option B: pre-filled or data-assisted eligibility verification with simplified application. - Option C: proactive or semi-automatic enrolment using verified administrative data with stronger safeguards and review controls.
  4. This synthetic case is a planning anchor only. JRC may replace it, but the control logic below should survive topic changes.
  5. Final scoring, weighting, trade-off choice, and policy framing remain human-owned even in the demonstrator.

RTM-01 Deliver one integrated package containing the methodological framework and the proof-of-concept demonstrator. Final submission must read as one coherent package with shared control logic, not as separate documents. Use ART-01 as the package spine and ensure every demonstrator component maps back into the methodology narrative and annexes. ART-01, ART-13, ART-14, ART-16 Planned Final integrated package with annex links and manifest Confirm whether JRC prefers one compiled document plus annex folder, or a single bundled dossier.
RTM-02 Submit in English and in electronic form. All controlled artifacts should be plain-language, reviewable, and exportable without specialist software. Keep all working artifacts in English markdown/table form and plan a final electronic export set. RTM-03 Complete by 22 May 2026 with room for JRC review and written comments. Internal freeze dates must precede the formal end date. Reserve one internal freeze for method pages, one for demonstrator pack, and one for comment incorporation. ART-01, ART-15, ART-16 Planned Milestone tracker, version history, comment log Exact interim milestone dates still need assignment.
RTM-04 Work in close coordination with JRC Unit S.3 using short review loops and clear checkpoints. The project needs explicit checkpoint gates rather than ad hoc circulation. Proposed checkpoint sequence: scope baseline -> synthetic case review -> intervention/gate review -> demonstrator walkthrough review -> final package review. ART-03, ART-04, ART-15 In draft Meeting records, decision notes, checkpoint approvals Need named JRC counterpart(s) and expected review cadence.

ART-03 now defines named intervention points from source ingestion to audit-pack assembly. ART-03 Ready for review Final intervention register cross-referenced to logs and walkthrough Confirm whether any additional intervention point is desired for stakeholder-input coding.
RTM-06 For each intervention point specify what AI may do, may not do, who reviews, and what record is kept. Each intervention point must have operational controls, not principle-only wording. For each intervention point, record allowed task, prohibited task, human reviewer, output artifact, logging fields, quality check, and escalation trigger. ART-03, ART-11, ART-12 Ready for review Completed register plus sample logs from demonstrator Need final log storage location and retention expectation.
RTM-07 AI may help organise evidence, draft structured notes, identify gaps or inconsistencies, and prepare review-ready documentation. AI scope is preparatory and documentary. Constrain demonstrator tasks to evidence-sheet drafting, criteria-draft support, rationale-note drafting, gap flagging, summary drafting, and audit-pack assembly. ART-03, ART-07, ART-08, ART-09, ART-13 In draft Sample filled evidence sheets, rationale notes, and review summary Need final model/tool choice for the proof of concept.
RTM-08 AI must not replace expert judgement, stakeholder consultation, analytical choice, scoring, trade-offs, or policy decision-making. Human decision rights are hard boundaries and must be visible in workflow and outputs. Explicitly prohibit AI-led scoring, weighting, ranking, trade-off resolution, and final recommendation text in the demonstrator. ART-03, ART-04, ART-13 Ready for review Prohibition statements, approved workflow, reviewer sign-offs Need standard disclaimer text for draft outputs.
RTM-09 Focus on preparatory work before final option comparison and keep uncertainty, disagreement, and missing evidence visible. The method must strengthen preparation without manufacturing false closure. Require uncertainty fields in evidence sheets, rationale notes, summary outputs, and ART-10. Seed the synthetic case with contradictory and incomplete evidence. ART-07, ART-09, ART-10, ART-13 Planned Completed uncertainty register and artifacts showing caveats Need agreement on minimum uncertainty categories to display in the final package narrative.
RTM-10 Every important AI output is draft until approved by a human reviewer. Draft status must be operational, not rhetorical. Use version-state flow: AI draft -> analyst revised -> under review -> approved for next step -> accepted for package. ART-04, ART-12 Ready for review Approval log showing status transitions by artifact version Need exact version naming convention.
RTM-11 No analytical output is accepted unless its evidential basis can be identified. Evidence traceability is mandatory for every substantive output. Require source IDs on evidence sheets and downstream references in rationale notes and summaries. No orphan analytical statements allowed. ART-06, ART-07, ART-09, ART-12 Planned Traceability spot checks from source to summary Need citation granularity decision: document-level vs section-level vs excerpt-level.
RTM-12 Demonstrator must keep a simple record of prompts/actions, revisions, and approvals; everything logged and trail stored and available. Logging should be lightweight but auditable and replayable. Use ART-11 for prompt/action records and ART-12 for review/approval events, both keyed to artifact IDs and versions. ART-11, ART-12, ART-16 Planned End-to-end action trail and manifest reconciliation Need storage/access method for logs and any redaction rules.
RTM-13 Use a synthetic use case rather than a live EC file. Demonstrator should be realistic but non-operational and non-sensitive. Use the provisional household energy-support access case as planning anchor until JRC confirms or substitutes another topic. ART-05, ART-06, ART-13 Planned Synthetic case note, option descriptions, evidence corpus Working case topic still provisional pending JRC confirmation.
RTM-14 Demonstrator is illustrative, not a production system; no complex integration assumed unless requested. Minimal architecture is preferable if it keeps workflow transparent. Keep demonstrator file-based with controlled inputs, generated outputs, and human review checkpoints. No EC system integration assumed. ART-13, ART-16 Planned Walkthrough script, run instructions, file inventory Need decision on local environment vs controlled hosted environment.
RTM-15 Align with Better Regulation principles and JRC working practice. Outputs should fit evidence-led impact-assessment work and downstream scrutiny. Keep problem framing, criteria logic, stakeholder visibility, uncertainty handling, and reviewability explicit in every core artifact. ART-05, ART-08, ART-10, ART-14 Planned Crosswalk narrative in integrated package and evaluation memo Need final statement on which Better Regulation elements are demonstrated directly vs referenced as context.
RTM-16 Provide transparency, human oversight, auditability, versioning, and proportionate use of explainable AI tasks. Governance features are core content, not annex-only compliance notes. Use ART-03 and ART-04 as core governance pages and force all demonstrator outputs through logged version and approval steps. ART-03, ART-04, ART-11, ART-12 Ready for review Governance chapter plus sample logged artifacts Need final role names once team composition is fixed.
RTM-17 Compliance-oriented design should already be compatible with stronger AI-governance expectations such as risk management, traceability, oversight, robustness, cybersecurity, and AI literacy. Even for a demonstrator, the package should show disciplined controls and operational hygiene. Include an explicit non-production environment note, log completeness rule, reviewer role separation, and short user instructions on appropriate AI use. ART-04, ART-13, ART-14 Planned Governance note, demonstrator runbook, evaluator findings Need proportionate cybersecurity and AI-literacy wording for a prototype context.
RTM-18 Accommodate JRC review and written comments. Comment handling must be structured and preserved. Use ART-15 to capture comment source, decision, action owner, revision reference, and closure status. ART-15 Planned Completed comment-resolution log linked to revised versions Need to know whether JRC prefers tracked comments, email consolidation, or meeting-based issue lists.
RTM-19 JRC intellectual-property ownership is accepted. Artifact design should avoid avoidable licensing ambiguity and dependence on non-transferable components. Maintain a dependency note for any external tool or model used; prefer outputs that remain fully transferrable to JRC. ART-13, ART-16 Planned Dependency list and final delivery note If external APIs/models are used, terms need explicit checking.
RTM-20 Lump-sum payment is tied to acceptance. Acceptance readiness should be demonstrable before submission. ART-04 will define release gates and ART-16 will list the exact accepted package contents. ART-04, ART-16 In draft Final acceptance checklist, sign-off memo, release manifest Full contract wording may still be needed to confirm the exact acceptance clause.

Proposed acceptance evidence set

  • Integrated package assembled under ART-01
  • Controlled artifact set ART-02 through ART-16
  • At least 6 completed evidence sheets from the synthetic corpus
  • At least 1 draft and 1 revised criteria structure note
  • At least 6 rationale notes across baseline and options
  • One populated uncertainty register with at least 5 material entries
  • One full demonstrator run with prompt/action log and approval log
  • One evaluator memo using the demonstrator evaluation criteria

Immediate decisions to request from JRC

  1. Confirm or replace the provisional synthetic case topic.
  2. Confirm preferred review cadence and which checkpoints require direct JRC participation.
  3. Confirm acceptable proof-of-concept model/tool environment.
  4. Confirm expected final delivery packaging and editable-source expectations.
  5. Confirm log storage and whether logs remain annexed or separate.

Main unresolved items in this draft

  • Synthetic case topic is still a working assumption.
  • Named people are not yet assigned to reviewer roles.
  • Log storage, redaction, and retention are still undefined.
  • Final export formats and acceptance packaging are not yet confirmed.
  • Better Regulation crosswalk is implied but not yet formalized as a standalone annex.
