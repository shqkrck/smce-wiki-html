AI intervention-point register

Document metadata

  • Document type: Project execution page
  • Status: Working operational control register
  • Scope: Bounded AI use within the SMCE preparation workflow for the JRC proof of concept
  • Primary sources:
  • AI-enabled SMCE technical proposal
  • Next steps after contract reread

What this page is for

  • Defines where AI is allowed to intervene in the workflow.
  • Sets the operational boundary for each intervention point.
  • Gives reviewers a single place to check what the AI is allowed to do, what it is forbidden to do, what evidence must be logged, and when escalation is required.

Operating rules that apply to every intervention point

  • All AI outputs are draft until a named human reviewer approves them.
  • No output is accepted unless the evidential basis can be identified.
  • Uncertainty, disagreement, and missing evidence must remain visible.
  • Prompts/actions, revisions, and approvals must be logged.
  • AI may support preparation and documentation only; it may not make final policy, scoring, weighting, or trade-off decisions.
  • If evidence is weak, contradictory, or missing, the workflow escalates to human review rather than forcing a false resolution.

Minimum logging fields for every intervention

  • Intervention ID
  • Date/time
  • Operator
  • Input artifact(s)
  • Prompt or action summary
  • Tool/model used
  • Output artifact ID/version
  • Reviewer
  • Review outcome
  • Quality issues found
  • Escalation triggered Y/N
  • Links to evidence sources

Role shorthand used in this register

  • Analyst / preparer: runs the step, checks basic factual fit, and assembles artifacts.
  • Methodological reviewer: checks methodological consistency, evidence traceability, and compliance with the governance rules.
  • Policy owner / case lead: decides whether the output is usable for the case context and whether unresolved issues are acceptable.
  • Validation sign-off role: approves inclusion in the final accepted package.

Operational constraints by task type

Allowed AI support pattern

  • Structure source material into repeatable templates.
  • Draft clearly marked working text for human review.
  • Surface gaps, inconsistency, duplication, and missing-evidence problems.
  • Help assemble review-ready summaries from already reviewed inputs.

Prohibited AI support pattern

  • Final scoring, weighting, ranking, trade-off resolution, or policy selection.
  • Replacing stakeholder consultation or expert judgement.
  • Treating uncertain or contradictory evidence as settled fact.
  • Producing unlogged substantive outputs for use in the final package.
  • Acting on live EC operational files within this proof of concept.

Standard quality-review checklist

  • Is the output still labelled as draft?
  • Can every substantive claim be traced to a source or prior approved artifact?
  • Are uncertainty, disagreement, and missing evidence visible?
  • Has the AI introduced unsupported interpretation or compression bias?
  • Is the artifact fit for the next gate, or does it require rework?
  • Is the log complete enough for replay and audit?

Escalation types

  • Method escalation: criteria logic unclear, artifact structure unstable, or workflow ambiguity detected.
  • Evidence escalation: source conflict, missing evidence, low-confidence claim, or traceability break.
  • Governance escalation: missing logs, unapproved reuse of AI draft text, or reviewer-role ambiguity.
  • Scope escalation: request would move the demonstrator toward live deployment, production integration, or autonomous judgement.

Exit conditions for an intervention point

An intervention point is considered complete only when:

  • the output artifact exists and has a version ID,
  • the required logs are complete,
  • the designated reviewer has recorded approve / reject / rework,
  • open issues are either escalated or accepted explicitly,
  • the next step knows which artifact version it is allowed to use.
